# Reporting Foreign Income

If you earn money outside your country of tax residence, here's what you need to know.

---

## US Citizens / Green Card Holders

The US taxes worldwide income regardless of where you live. If you're a US citizen working in London, you owe the IRS a return even if you owe UK tax on the same income.

### Avoiding Double Taxation

**Option 1: Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)**
- Credit the foreign tax you paid against your US tax liability
- Dollar-for-dollar credit (with limitations)
- Generally better for high earners or those in high-tax countries (UK, Canada, most of Europe)
- Can carry forward unused credits for up to 10 years

**Option 2: Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (Form 2555)**
- Exclude up to ~$126,500 (2024) of foreign earned income
- Must meet the physical presence test (330 days abroad in 12-month period) OR bona fide residence test
- Cannot exclude investment income, only earned income
- Once you revoke the FEIE election, you can't re-elect for 5 years

**Which to choose?**
| Situation | Better Choice |
|-----------|--------------|
| Foreign tax rate > US rate | Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) |
| Foreign tax rate < US rate | May be FEIE or FTC — run both calculations |
| High earned income (> $126K) | FTC (FEIE caps out) |
| Moderate income, low-tax country | FEIE |
| Investment income from abroad | FTC (FEIE doesn't cover investment income) |
| UK, Canada, most of EU | Almost always FTC |

### Foreign Housing Exclusion
- Additional exclusion for qualifying housing expenses abroad
- Above a base amount (~$18K, varies by location)
- Up to ~$37K additional exclusion (varies by location — IRS publishes city-specific limits)
- Must meet same physical presence / bona fide residence test as FEIE

## Reporting Requirements

### FBAR (FinCEN 114)
- **Who:** Any US person with foreign financial accounts exceeding $10,000 aggregate at ANY point during the year
- **What counts:** Bank accounts, investment accounts, pension accounts, signature authority accounts
- **What doesn't count:** Foreign real estate (directly held), foreign stock (held in US brokerage)
- **Where to file:** BSA E-Filing System (NOT with your tax return)
- **Deadline:** April 15, auto-extended to October 15
- **Penalty:** Up to $10,000/account/year (non-willful), $100,000+ or 50% of balance (willful)

### FATCA (Form 8938)
- **Who:** US persons with specified foreign financial assets above threshold
- **Thresholds (living abroad):** $200,000 at year-end or $300,000 at any point
- **Thresholds (living in US):** $50,000 at year-end or $75,000 at any point
- **What counts:** Bank accounts, investment accounts, foreign stock/securities held outside US institution, interests in foreign entities, foreign financial instruments
- **Where to file:** Attached to your Form 1040
- **Overlap with FBAR:** Yes, you may need to report the same accounts on both

### Form 5471 (Foreign Corporation)
- **Who:** US persons who own 10%+ of a Controlled Foreign Corporation
- **What it does:** Reports income, assets, and activities of the foreign corporation
- **Penalty:** $10,000 per form per year for late/non-filing
- **Key concept:** Subpart F income — certain types of passive income earned by a CFC are taxed to the US shareholder currently, even if not distributed

### Form 8865 (Foreign Partnership)
- **Who:** US persons with interests in foreign partnerships (varies by category)
- **Penalty:** $10,000 per form per year

## UK-Specific for US Citizens

### UK Employment Income
- Your UK employer withholds PAYE (income tax) and National Insurance
- Report all UK income on US Form 1040
- Claim FTC on Form 1116 for PAYE and NI contributions
- UK marginal rates often exceed US rates, so you may owe $0 US tax on UK employment income

### UK Pension (Workplace)
- UK employer pension contributions may not be deductible on US return by default
- Treaty election (Article 18) may allow deferral — consult international CPA
- When you withdraw from UK pension, US taxes it as income (with FTC for UK tax paid)

### UK Individual Savings Account (ISA)
- Tax-free in UK
- NOT tax-free for US persons — US taxes ISA gains/interest annually
- Consider not using ISAs if you're a US citizen (the US tax cost eliminates the UK benefit)

## Canada-Specific for US Citizens

### RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)
- Canada's equivalent of 401(k)
- Treaty provision allows US deferral (elect on Form 8891 — now automatic under Rev. Proc. 2014-55)
- Contributions may not be deductible on US return

### TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)
- Tax-free in Canada
- NOT tax-free for US persons — similar problem to UK ISAs
- May be treated as a foreign trust (Form 3520/3520-A reporting)
- Generally recommended to NOT use TFSAs if you're a US citizen

### RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan)
- Canada's 529 equivalent
- May be treated as foreign trust for US purposes
- Reporting complexity makes it problematic for US citizens

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not filing at all** because "I paid tax in the other country" — you still need to file
2. **Using FEIE when FTC is better** — run both calculations
3. **Forgetting FBAR** — this is a separate filing from your tax return
4. **Not reporting foreign pension** — even if no US tax is due
5. **Using foreign tax-advantaged accounts** (ISAs, TFSAs) as a US citizen — often worse after US tax treatment
6. **Not timing elections properly** — some elections (like FEIE) are hard to undo
7. **Ignoring state tax** — some US states (California, New York) tax former residents aggressively
