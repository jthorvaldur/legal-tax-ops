# How to Properly Dissolve an LLC You Forgot About

If you formed an LLC and stopped using it, it's still alive — and potentially accumulating fees and penalties. Here's how to clean it up.

---

## Step 1: Find It

Search the Secretary of State website in every state you might have formed an entity:

| State | Search URL |
|-------|-----------|
| Wyoming | wyobiz.wyo.gov/Business/FilingSearch.aspx |
| Delaware | icis.corp.delaware.gov/ecorp/entitysearch/namesearch.aspx |
| California | bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/search/business |
| Texas | mycpa.cpa.state.tx.us/coa |
| Nevada | esos.nv.gov/EntitySearch/OnlineEntitySearch |
| Illinois | ilsos.gov/corporatellc |
| New York | appext20.dos.ny.gov/corp_public/CORPSEARCH.ENTITY_SEARCH_ENTRY |

Also search: your email for "articles of organization," "EIN confirmation," "annual report due."

## Step 2: Assess the Damage

Once you find the entity, determine:

| Question | How to Find Out |
|----------|----------------|
| Is it still active? | Secretary of State website — shows "Active" or "Administratively Dissolved" |
| Are there unpaid fees? | Secretary of State website or call their office |
| Did you file tax returns? | Check your records. If you got an EIN, the IRS expects returns. |
| Is there a bank account? | Check your bank records |
| Are there outstanding obligations? | Contracts, debts, liabilities |

## Step 3: If Administratively Dissolved

Many states automatically dissolve entities after 2-3 years of non-compliance. This is NOT the same as a proper dissolution.

**Options:**
- **Let it stay dissolved** — in some states this is fine. Outstanding fees may still be owed.
- **Reinstate then dissolve** — some states require reinstatement before formal dissolution. This usually means paying back fees.
- **Check with the state** — call the Secretary of State's office and ask what's required

## Step 4: File Final Tax Returns

If you obtained an EIN and haven't been filing:

| Situation | What to Do |
|-----------|-----------|
| Single-member LLC, no revenue | File a zero Schedule C for each missing year |
| Multi-member LLC, no activity | File zero Form 1065 for each missing year ($220/partner/month penalty possible) |
| S-Corp election was made | File zero Form 1120-S for each missing year ($220/shareholder/month penalty) |
| State income tax returns | File zero returns for each missing year |

**Penalty abatement:** First-time penalty abatement (FTA) may eliminate one year of penalties. Reasonable cause may eliminate more. Call the IRS at (800) 829-4933.

## Step 5: Formally Dissolve

1. **File Articles of Dissolution** (or Certificate of Cancellation) with the Secretary of State
2. **Pay any outstanding fees** — most states require this before accepting dissolution
3. **Mark final tax returns as "FINAL"** — check the "final return" box on each form

## Step 6: Close the EIN

The IRS doesn't technically "close" EINs, but you can deactivate the business account:

1. Send a letter to:
   ```
   Internal Revenue Service
   Cincinnati, OH 45999
   ```
2. Include: EIN, entity name, reason for closing, your signature
3. Or call: (800) 829-4933

## Step 7: Clean Up

- [ ] Close any bank accounts associated with the entity
- [ ] Cancel registered agent service
- [ ] Notify any remaining business contacts
- [ ] Save all dissolution documents for 7+ years
- [ ] Update your entity registry (see `templates/entity-registry.md`)

## State-Specific Costs of Catching Up

| State | Back Fees Typical | Dissolution Fee | Pain Level |
|-------|------------------|----------------|------------|
| Wyoming | $60/year missed | $0 | Low |
| Delaware | $300/year + $200 late fee/year | $0 | Medium |
| California | $800/year (franchise tax!) + penalties | $0 | High |
| Nevada | $150/year + $75 late/year | $0 | Medium |
| New York | $9/year (biennial) | $0 | Low |
| Texas | ~$0 if under franchise tax threshold | $0 | Low |

**California is the worst:** If you formed a CA LLC 5 years ago and forgot about it, you may owe $4,000+ in franchise taxes alone, even with zero revenue.

---

## Prevention

After cleaning up, prevent this from happening again:
1. Use `templates/entity-registry.md` to track all entities
2. Set annual calendar reminders for each entity
3. Don't form entities you don't need
4. Dissolve immediately when an entity is no longer needed
5. Use low-cost states (Wyoming, New Mexico) if you're forming speculatively
