# Setting Up Your First LLC

A step-by-step guide to forming an LLC properly, from choosing a state to getting your EIN.

---

## Step 1: Choose Your State

| If You... | Best State | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Operate in one state | That state | Avoid dual registration fees |
| Want privacy + low cost | Wyoming | $60/year, no state income tax, strong privacy |
| Are raising VC money | Delaware | Investors and lawyers expect it |
| Don't care about state specifics | Your home state | Simplest compliance |

**Avoid California** unless you must operate there — $800/year minimum franchise tax even with $0 revenue.

## Step 2: Choose a Name

- Must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
- Must be distinguishable from existing entities in the state
- Check availability on your state's Secretary of State website
- Consider: will you want this as a domain name?

## Step 3: Appoint a Registered Agent

Every LLC needs a registered agent in the state of formation — someone to receive legal notices.

| Option | Cost | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|------|
| Yourself | Free | No cost | Your address is public, you must be available during business hours |
| Northwest Registered Agent | $125/year | Good service, privacy, mail forwarding | Annual cost |
| LegalZoom Agent | $299/year | Bundle with formation | Expensive for what you get |
| Incfile | $119/year (free first year with formation) | Cheap | Basic service |

## Step 4: File Articles of Organization

File with the Secretary of State. Most states have online filing.

| State | Where | Fee |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Wyoming | [wyobiz.wyo.gov](https://wyobiz.wyo.gov) | $100 |
| Delaware | [corp.delaware.gov](https://corp.delaware.gov) | $90 |
| Texas | [sos.state.tx.us](https://www.sos.state.tx.us) | $300 |

**What you need:**
- LLC name
- Registered agent name and address
- Member/organizer name(s) and address(es)
- Purpose (most states accept "any lawful purpose")

## Step 5: Get an EIN

- **Where:** [IRS EIN Assistant](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online) (free, online, instant)
- **When:** After your Articles of Organization are filed and accepted
- **What you need:** SSN/ITIN of responsible party, entity information

## Step 6: Open a Bank Account

- Bring: EIN letter, Articles of Organization, operating agreement, government ID
- Many banks now allow online applications
- Keep business and personal finances completely separate

## Step 7: Create an Operating Agreement

Even if your state doesn't require it, you need one. It defines:
- Who owns what percentage
- How profits and losses are distributed
- How decisions are made
- What happens if a member leaves, dies, or goes through divorce
- How the LLC can be dissolved

Single-member LLCs need a simple one. Multi-member LLCs need a thorough one.

## Step 8: Set Up Bookkeeping

- Open QuickBooks, Xero, or Wave from day one
- Connect your bank account for auto-import
- Categorize every transaction
- This is not optional — you'll need clean books for taxes

## Step 9: Understand Your Tax Obligations

| LLC Type | Default Tax Treatment | Federal Filing |
|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Single-member | Disregarded entity (Schedule C on your 1040) | Form 1040 Schedule C |
| Multi-member | Partnership | Form 1065 + K-1s |
| S-Corp election | S-Corporation | Form 1120-S + K-1s |

**Don't elect S-Corp status** unless your CPA recommends it based on your specific revenue and salary situation. It adds complexity and payroll requirements.

## Step 10: Annual Compliance

Set calendar reminders for:
- [ ] Annual report filing (check your state's deadline)
- [ ] Franchise tax payment (if applicable)
- [ ] Federal tax return
- [ ] State tax return (if state has income tax)
- [ ] Registered agent renewal
- [ ] Business license renewal (if applicable)
- [ ] Bookkeeping reconciliation (monthly)
