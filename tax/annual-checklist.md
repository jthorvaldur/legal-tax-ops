# Annual Tax Operations Calendar

A month-by-month checklist for managing tax obligations across US, UK, and Canadian jurisdictions. Customize for your specific entities and situations.

---

## How to Use This

1. Copy this file and fill in your specific entities and deadlines
2. Set calendar reminders 30 days before each deadline
3. Review quarterly — situations change, entities are created/dissolved
4. Share with your tax professional so they know what's coming

---

## January

- [ ] **Review all entity statuses** — check Secretary of State websites for each entity
- [ ] **Gather W-2s, 1099s, K-1s** as they arrive (due to you by Jan 31)
- [ ] **UK Self Assessment deadline** — Jan 31 for prior tax year (ending April 5)
- [ ] **Review registered agent invoices** — are you paying for entities you should dissolve?
- [ ] **Start organizing receipts** for deductible expenses
- [ ] **Check FBAR threshold** — did any foreign account exceed $10K at any point last year?

## February

- [ ] **Receive all tax documents** — W-2s, 1099s, K-1s should be in hand
- [ ] **Contact tax professionals** — schedule appointments early (they fill up)
- [ ] **Organize documents by entity** — separate pile for each LLC/entity
- [ ] **Canada T4/T5 slips** arrive (Canadian employment/investment income)
- [ ] **Review estimated tax payments made** — do they match what was required?

## March

- [ ] **Prepare returns or prepare extension requests** for all entities
- [ ] **Multi-member LLC K-1s** must be issued to partners by March 15
- [ ] **Form 1065 (partnership returns)** due March 15 (or extension)
- [ ] **S-Corp returns (1120-S)** due March 15 (or extension)
- [ ] **State annual reports** — check which states have March deadlines

## April

- [ ] **April 15 — US Individual tax return (1040)** due (or extension)
- [ ] **April 15 — FBAR (FinCEN 114)** due (auto-extended to Oct 15)
- [ ] **April 15 — Estimated tax payment Q1** (if applicable)
- [ ] **April 30 — Canadian T1** due (June 15 if self-employed, but payment still due April 30)
- [ ] **State income tax returns** — most due April 15
- [ ] **UK tax year starts April 6** — new tax year begins

## May

- [ ] **File extensions if needed** — if you filed a US extension, you have until October 15
- [ ] **Review Q1 business performance** — adjust estimated payments if needed
- [ ] **Entity annual reports** — check for May deadlines in your states

## June

- [ ] **June 15 — US citizens abroad** — automatic 2-month extension expires (file or extend)
- [ ] **June 15 — Estimated tax payment Q2**
- [ ] **June 15 — Canadian T1** for self-employed individuals
- [ ] **Review mid-year tax position** — are you on track? Over/under-withheld?

## July

- [ ] **UK P11D deadline** — July 6 (benefits in kind reporting)
- [ ] **Entity annual reports** — check for July deadlines
- [ ] **Mid-year entity audit** — any entities to create or dissolve?

## August - September

- [ ] **September 15 — Estimated tax payment Q3**
- [ ] **Partnership/S-Corp extension deadline** — September 15
- [ ] **Begin year-end tax planning** — timing of income, deductions, entity elections
- [ ] **Review retirement contributions** — maximize before year-end

## October

- [ ] **October 15 — Extended 1040 deadline** (absolute deadline for individual returns)
- [ ] **October 15 — FBAR final deadline** (auto-extended from April 15)
- [ ] **October 15 — FATCA (Form 8938)** extended deadline
- [ ] **File any remaining extended returns**
- [ ] **UK Self Assessment registration** — register by Oct 5 if new to self-assessment

## November - December

- [ ] **Year-end tax planning** — harvest losses, make charitable contributions, time income
- [ ] **Review entity structures** — is your current structure still optimal?
- [ ] **Maximize retirement contributions** before Dec 31 (except IRA, which has until April 15)
- [ ] **January 15 — Estimated tax payment Q4** (due in January but plan in December)
- [ ] **UK Self Assessment** — begin preparing (due Jan 31)
- [ ] **Document all foreign accounts** — prepare for FBAR filing
- [ ] **Review insurance, benefits, FSA** — use-it-or-lose-it items

---

## Recurring Monthly Tasks

- [ ] Reconcile all business bank accounts
- [ ] Categorize all business expenses
- [ ] File any required state sales tax returns
- [ ] Review upcoming deadlines (30-day lookahead)
- [ ] Save 25-35% of any 1099/freelance income for taxes

## Penalty Reference

| Missed Item | Penalty |
|------------|---------|
| Late 1040 (with tax owed) | 5% of unpaid tax per month, up to 25% |
| Late 1040 (no tax owed) | $0 (but file anyway) |
| Late FBAR | $10,000 per account per year (non-willful); up to $100K or 50% of balance (willful) |
| Late FATCA (8938) | $10,000 + $10,000/month after 90 days, up to $50,000 |
| Late Form 5471 | $10,000 per form per year |
| Late Form 1065 (partnership) | $220/partner/month, up to 12 months |
| Late Canadian T1 | 5% of balance + 1% per month, up to 12 months |
| Late UK Self Assessment | £100 flat + daily penalties after 3 months + % penalties after 6 and 12 months |
